Breathing & Calm

Breathe your way out of stress anywhere.

These techniques work while parked, during a break, in your sleeper, or before a stressful delivery. Simple breathing can help calm your nervous system within minutes.

60 seconds • Anxiety & stress

The 4-7-8 Breath

A simple breathing pattern that helps signal your body to slow down.

  • 1Exhale completely through your mouth.
  • 2Inhale through your nose for 4 counts.
  • 3Hold your breath for 7 counts.
  • 4Exhale slowly for 8 counts.
  • 5Repeat 3 to 4 cycles.
Use this before a difficult delivery, after a near-miss, or when you feel stress building.
4 minutes • Focus reset

Box Breathing

  • 1Inhale for 4 counts.
  • 2Hold for 4 counts.
  • 3Exhale for 4 counts.
  • 4Hold empty for 4 counts.
30 seconds • Instant relief

Physiological Sigh

  • 1Take a normal inhale through your nose.
  • 2At the top, sniff in once more.
  • 3Exhale slowly and fully through your mouth.
A calm body makes better decisions on the road.

Sleep Optimization

Sleep like your license depends on it.

Poor sleep affects blood pressure, reaction time, cravings, weight, and mood. These steps are built for sleeper berths, irregular hours, and noisy truck stops.

Under 2 minutes • In your berth

Military Sleep Method

  • 1Relax your face, jaw, cheeks, and forehead.
  • 2Drop your shoulders and release your arms.
  • 3Exhale and relax your chest.
  • 4Release your legs from thighs to feet.
  • 5Clear your mind with one calm image.
One-time setup • Major impact

Optimize Your Sleeper Berth

  • •Keep it cool when possible.
  • •Block light with curtains or an eye mask.
  • •Use white noise or earplugs.
  • •Turn phone notifications off before sleep.
20 minutes • HOS break

The 20-Minute Reset

A short nap can restore alertness without leaving you groggy. Keep it under 30 minutes.

Better sleep protects your health, focus, and CDL.
Cab-Friendly Fitness

Move without a gym, mat, or extra time.

Every routine here fits into the time drivers already have: fuel stops, HOS breaks, rest stops, and dock waits.

8 minutes • Every fuel stop

The Parking Lot Protocol

  • 1Walk 3 laps around your truck.
  • 2Do 10 squats.
  • 3Do 10 push-ups against your bumper or step.
  • 4Do 10 calf raises.
  • 5Stretch each hip flexor for 30 seconds.
Calf raises and walking help circulation after long sitting periods.
5 minutes • While parked

Seated Cab Exercises

  • •Seated torso twists.
  • •Knee raises.
  • •Shoulder rolls.
  • •Ankle circles.
25 minutes • 30-minute break

Full HOS Break Workout

Walk, squat, push, lunge, stretch, and still have time left to eat or hydrate.

Small movement, repeated often, changes everything.
Mental Health on the Road

The hardest miles are not always on the road.

Isolation, time away from family, financial pressure, and physical strain add up quietly. These tools are practical, private, and built for real drivers.

5 minutes • Every day

The Daily Connection Rule

  • 1Pick one person.
  • 2Call at the same time each day.
  • 3Make it a voice call, not just a text.
  • 4Talk about something besides work.
2 minutes • Road rage reset

Cognitive Reframe

  • 1Name the trigger out loud.
  • 2Take one physiological sigh.
  • 3Ask, “Will this matter in an hour?”
  • 4Replace the thought with a calmer one.
Know the signs

When to Get Real Help

If you feel hopeless, numb, detached, or have thoughts of hurting yourself, call or text 988 for immediate support.

Connection is health care too.
